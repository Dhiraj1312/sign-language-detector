Deaf individuals experience hearing loss, while non-verbal people face challenges in speech expression. Sign language, a visual-gestural communication method, is pivotal in bridging these gaps Learning and promoting sign language enable effective communication and inclusivity. Providing accessible resources, fostering understanding, and embracing diverse communication methods empower these individuals to navigate a world that might not always cater to their specific needs.

Our project focuses on empowering individuals with speech impairments, specifically those who use hand sign language as their primary means of communication.

We used technology that enables computers to interpret and respond to human hand movements. It typically involves a camera capturing the hand gestures, which are then processed using computer vision algorithms. These algorithms analyze key features like finger positions, palm orientation, and movement patterns. Machine learning models are often employed to classify these features and associate them with specific gestures. The recognized gestures can then trigger predefined actions, making it a valuable interface in applications like virtual reality, gaming, and human-computer interaction.
